A poor eater

The person with dementia can eat less than before, perhaps it’s because they no longer remember to eat. It can also be because they no longer remember how to use a knife and fork or that they don’t recognize the food. This is not unusual and can be a result of the disease.

What shall I do?

- Create peace and quiet during meals. Turn off the TV, radio or other sources of sound.

- Reduce impressions and stimuli. Only put on the table what is going to be used during the meal e.g. the right cutlery and one piece at a time. Too many things on the table can cause confusion.

- Serve food in an appetizing way. Use contrasting colours e.g. a coloured plate if serving white fish. This will make it easier to see the food.

- Avoid asking questions or talking too much, this will make it easier to concentrate on eating.

- Offer a snack like a piece of fruit, biscuits, ice-cream or a sandwich several times a day.

- Remember that it’s important with regular visits to the toilet.

- If necessary contact and visit a dentist. Pain from sores, fungi or a damaged tooth may be causing the problem.

- Do the medicines need to be reviewed? Some medicines can have an effect on both taste and appetite. Contact the health centre and talk to a doctor about a drug review.
