Calling out and shouting

A person with dementia can shout out loud or scream. They may call for help or for their mother.

To hear someone shouting or screaming can be unpleasant, frightening and tough. It can be a way of saying that they feel abandoned, but it can also be a reflex reaction. The person who is ill is not always conscious of the fact that they are shouting or screaming.

What shall I do?

- Ask a nurse or a doctor to examine them to see if the shouting is due to pain or something else unpleasant.

- Reduce the number of impressions and stimuli. A damaged brain needs more rest than a healthy brain. Suggest a nap in the middle of the day.

- Help them to take part in things according to their ability, everything from combing their hair to mixing or beating a cake.

- Go for a walk or do something else that will distract them.

- Listen to the person's favourite music, perhaps with headphones.

- Be close by and have some sort of physical contact.

- Fuss over them e.g. by rubbing in perfumed skin cream, fix their hair, cut their nails or do something else that they like.
