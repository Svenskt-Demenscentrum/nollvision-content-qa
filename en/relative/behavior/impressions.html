Minimize impressions

A person with dementia has difficulty in interpreting stimuli. Sound can seem louder and depth perception can deteriorate. A person can have difficulty interpreting and finding their way around in their surroundings. Stimuli can be difficult to sort out and put in a logical context. The brain has to work at high speed. You can help the person with dementia by adapting the environment. Try to limit stimuli and create a calm environment.

Sudden loud noises can be frightening. Turn down disturbing sounds from e.g. TV, radio or kitchen machines.

Limit visual stimuli, e.g. at mealtimes only take up what is going to be used. If there are too many things there is a risk that your relative will mix things up. Contrasting colours can be used to make it easier to perceive

Dark colours, areas such as floors or mats can be interpreted as being submerged in relationship to lighter areas. It can be a good idea to remove a dark mat that perhaps is seen as a ditch or a hole.

The sense of touch is what develops first and remains throughout life. Physical contact can create peace and quiet and give a feeling of wellbeing.
