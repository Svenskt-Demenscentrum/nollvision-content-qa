Strengthen what is healthy

Most people want to feel that they belong and that they make a contribution. This is also the case even if one has dementia and are dependent upon help. Otherwise there is a risk that one avoids contact, become passive and rapidly lose different capabilities.

What shall I do?

  • Try and not take over too much, even if the person with dementia isnâ€™t fully capable of doing ordinary everyday household activities.
  • Consolidate/Build on what is healthy and let them do what they are still capable of doing: make the bed, peel potatoes, collect the post, shop or be in the garden.
  • At a later stage of the disease walks, reading aloud, music and being in different social environments can create a feeling of belonging.
  • Being near to someone and physical contact or being given help with hair and nails can also give a feeling of wellbeing.
  • Contact your needs assessor to find out about possible day activities with different sorts of social and physical activity.
