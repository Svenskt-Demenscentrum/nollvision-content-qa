What is deemed as restraint or force?

Restraining a person

A relative or member of the nursing staff restraining and holding on to a person against their will.

Locking the door

Most people lock the door at home. But locking someone in can be seen as restraint, even if the purpose is to protect. If the person suffering from dementia cannot be at home alone, extra help at home can be a solution.

Physical restraints

Using a belt or a table to stop someone from getting up is regarded as restraint.

Medication

Using routine medication to keep a person calm can be a coercive measure. If a person is angry or anxious, other reasons for this, e.g. an infection or pain, must first be ruled out before considering giving sedatives.
