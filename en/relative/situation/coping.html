Coping

“I went home from the doctor with my father in one hand and the Alzheimer diagnosis in the other. I thought: How shall I cope with this….

A dementia illness changes not only the life of the one who is ill. A relationship is no longer what it was before and it can take some time to find one’s roll as a relative. To take care of and treat with respect and patience can be a difficult task.

Many feel guilty for not being sufficient. As a carer, being angry, filled with sorrow, frustration and tiredness is natural and very common.
