If restraint is used

There is no legal support for force or restraint. Sometimes they may seem necessary as the last resort and as a temporary solution in a serious and acute situation. In these cases, the ambition must be to cease using force as soon as possible.

  • Staffing and supervision. If possible increase the number of staff. Provide regular supervision and attention.
  • Stop using force or restraint. Evaluate possible risks for the person, other residents, visitors and staff. Stop using force or restraint as soon as possible.
  • Identify the trigger mechanisms. What could have led up to the situation? Discuss this with colleagues, with relatives and if possible with the person concerned. Think about how the behaviour can be prevented.
  • Documentation and interaction strategies. Document the situation and the interaction strategies that can be used if the situation arises again. Revise the social and medical records so that everyone works in the same way.
  • File a report on adverse incidents or injuries while in care. Report, at first-hand, to your supervisor if something happens, e.g. you have to take hold of, restrain, a person who is about to hit another person.
