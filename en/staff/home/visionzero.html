Vision zero – how?

How can we work towards a dementia care without restraint? A work place policy is a good tool to have to enable the implementation of a vision zero. Remember that creating routines and implementing them is a continuous ongoing process.

Here are some tips and advice.

Information

How can my colleagues and I make sure that everyone is informed about the vision zero?

- Discuss what a vision zero entails at every staff meeting, in order to implement work procedures.

- Inform new colleagues about what a vision zero policy implies at your workplace. Give examples on how you work in a preventive manner to hinder difficult behavioural situations from arising.

Person-centred care

How can my colleagues and I work in a person-centred way?

- A life history should be documented for all those who give their consent. It is a good tool in helping to understand and handle behavioural problems.

- With good knowledge about each person, individual activities can be planned and documented so that everyone knows what to do.

Checklist Dementia (only available in Swedish) can be used as a tool for following up each person, according to the National Board for Health and Welfare’s guidelines for dementia care.

Prevention

How can we stay one step ahead and work in a preventive manner in order to avoid behaviour that creates difficult situations?

- Make sure that routines are available for each profession. Who does what in a mapping and how is information shared between colleagues?

- Establish routines for those responsible for regular assessments of the physical and psychosocial environment.

- Make sure that the preventative methods are documented and that they are within easy reach.

- Follow up the methods of prevention at regular staff meetings.

Interaction strategies

In spite of careful mapping of trigger mechanisms and working in a preventive manner, it can be difficult to completely prevent difficult situations from arising. What shall I do if difficult situations still arise?

- At staff meetings, discuss with your colleagues the needs of the person with dementia based on the mappings and the interaction strategies that have been established.

- At staff reflection meetings, discuss situations where you see the need to evaluate and improve strategies e.g. shower or mealtime situations.

- Establish routines for what is to be done in acute situations e.g. if someone becomes aggressive and begins to fight. Who does what if a person disappears from the residency/nursing home? It is a good idea to establish a plan of action.

- Follow up interaction strategies regularly in your team. Do you follow the strategies? If not, why?
