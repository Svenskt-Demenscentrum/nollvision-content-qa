Consent

The basis of health care and/or social services is that it should be voluntary and that the person consents to the treatment offered. There are various forms of consent. Expressed consent means that a person, actively in writing, orally or by a consenting nod gives their consent to a particular form of treatment.

Another form of consent is Presumed consent. This means that whoever carries out the health and/or social care assumes that the treatment is in accordance with the personâ€™s wishes even if consent has not been specifically expressed.
