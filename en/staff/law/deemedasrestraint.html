What is deemed as restraint?

According to the law, all measures of care that are carried out against a person’s wishes are accounted as force or restraint. Here are examples of measures that can be deemed as restraint if the person does not give their consent:

  • Using bed rails, seatbelts or tables that prevent a person from moving freely.
  • Trying to control behaviour by holding on to a person.
  • Locking a door and preventing a person from going where they want to.
  • Using medication as a routine measure to sedate and keep a person calm.

Electronic measures such as video surveillance, GPS, passive alarms such as movement detectors or electronic sound surveillance can be restraining if they are used against someone’s wishes. If a person consents to these measures they can, instead, be good aids which lead to increased security and a sense of freedom.
