Power of attorney

General power of attorney

It is good to inform relatives of some of the practical issues when their next of kin is diagnosed with dementia. A general power of attorney will give the proxy owner the right to take care of practical matters. In this way a relative or a friend can pay the bills and also look after the personâ€™s interests. The power of attorney applies as long as the person with dementia has the ability to take in and understand information and communicate their own decisions.

Lasting power of attorney

When this no longer applies a lasting power of attorney can come in handy. It's content can be the some as in the general power of attorney but applies also when the person with dementia no longer can make informed decisions. It's a good idea to have the lasting power of attorney made att the some time as the general power of attorney.

Family mandate

In case there are no proxis of any kind, family members may still make for exemples pay bills and make tax returns for the person whith dementia. It is also a good idea to make sure that the person with dementia has legitimate identity papers.
