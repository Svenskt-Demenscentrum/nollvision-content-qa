Trustee and administrator

A trustee is like a personâ€™s extended arm. He or she can give both help in making decisions and support in different situations. The application must be sent to the District Court.

The one who needs help decides whether he or she wants a trustee and can at any point in time terminate the trusteeship. A medical certificate is needed if the person cannot speak for him/herself.

Basically, an administrator helps in the same way as a trustee, but to be given an administrator is not always voluntary. An administrator is designated by the District Court when a person needs to be protected from someone else or from her/himself.

Perhaps the person in question has difficulty in understanding or of knowing what is best for them. A person with an administrator can no longer sign contracts by him/herself or buy things and pay by instalments. An administrator does not need to get consent from the person in question.

There must be strong reasons and an investigation carried out before the District Court can make a decision on trusteeship.

No one, whether it is the trustee, administrator or next of kin, can make decisions regarding Social Services or Healthcare.
