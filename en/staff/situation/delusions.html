Delusions

"Elsa says that someone has taken her wallet and you feel accused of having taken it."

When suffering from dementia it is not unusual to have delusions. Believing that people hide or steal things, that someone has poisoned the food are some examples of delusion. A person might also believe that their partner is unfaithful, misinterpret their surroundings and also other people's behaviour.

Tips:

- Look for the wallet together. Start talking about something else after a while to distract her.

- Remember, a delusion is real for the person, even if you know that it’s not real. This makes it almost impossible to convince someone by using logic or rational explanations.

- Ask: “What’s disappeared? I’ll go and check it out.” Or: “I’ve written that down and I’ll take care of it”. In this way you take over the responsibility and perhaps she’ll calm down.

- If the delusions are difficult to cope with, contact a doctor for an assessment.

- Talk to your colleagues about how to handle the situation. Together you may be able to find a strategy that helps.
